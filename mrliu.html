Course Description

AP Computer Science Principles is a year course. It is a broad based principles course providing how computers and the logic and technology underneath are used in our everyday lives. There are two components of the AP Exam towards the end of the year. There is a Create Performance Task which requires you to demonstrate your mastery of computer programming. This task is worth 30% of your total AP Score. The second portion is the traditional multiplce choice exam. You will be provided 2 hours to answer 70 MCQs. This will be worth 70% of your AP score.

Big Ideas

  1. Creative Development
  2. Data
  3. Algorithms and Programming
  4. Computer Systems and Networks
  5. Impact of Computing
